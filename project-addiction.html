Project Overview

Figure 1 — Phone Usage and Addiction Level Dashboard (Power BI)

This project analyzes smartphone usage and addiction behavior using behavioral data collected from 5,075 users across the Predictive Smartphone Study S4. The aim is to uncover patterns in screen time, sleep deprivation, social media consumption, gaming habits, and notification overload, and to identify the key behavioral drivers that predict addiction levels (Low, Moderate, or High).

The analysis follows a full data science pipeline — from data cleaning and feature engineering in Python, through 27 structured SQL queries in MySQL, to an interactive Power BI dashboard — culminating in actionable digital wellness recommendations.

Research Question Can behavioral data — including daily screen time, social media hours, gaming, sleep, and notification patterns — accurately predict a user’s smartphone addiction level?

Feature Engineering

  • Age Group — 5-year bands: 18–22, 23–27, 28–32, 33–35
  • Screen Time Group — Low (<5h) / Moderate / High / Very High (11h+)
  • Sleep Band — Very Poor to Excellent based on sleep_hours
  • Notification Group — Low (<50) to Very High (175+)
  • Addiction Risk Score — (screen × 0.4) + (sleep deficit × 0.8) + (notif × 0.02) + (app opens × 0.01)

Key Insights

Finding 01
81.2% of users are Moderate addiction
The majority are at risk — not just a small extreme group
Finding 02
High users average 11.67h screen time daily
More than double the Low group — screen time is the key signal
Finding 03
Gender has no meaningful impact on addiction
All three genders show near-identical addiction splits
Finding 04
56.7% of all users are under-slept
Sleep deprivation is systemic, not isolated to high addiction
Finding 05
Notification counts are flat across addiction tiers
Notifications are not the root cause — total exposure is
Finding 06
Weekend screen time spikes across all groups
Unstructured free time accelerates phone use significantly
Finding 07
Risk score validates against addiction labels
Composite scoring supports early intervention flagging

Recommendations

Immediate Actions

  • Implement daily screen time caps at 8–10h range
  • Launch a sleep-first digital wellness campaign
  • Flag risk scores above 15 for in-app wellness nudges

Medium-Term Strategies

  • Target 18–22 age group with early intervention
  • Develop a Weekend Mode with usage reminders
  • Reframe strategy to focus on total screen time

Long-Term Goals

  • Build a machine learning addiction prediction model
  • Expand to longitudinal user tracking
  • Integrate with healthcare platforms

Smartphone addiction is driven by cumulative daily exposure, disrupted sleep, and unstructured weekend usage. Screen time — not gender, age, or notification volume — is the single most actionable lever for reducing addiction severity. The most effective interventions address all three drivers simultaneously through built-in platform tools, behavioral nudges, and sleep-aware design.
